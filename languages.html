Source: Wikipedia metadata

The languages you speak vastly affect which and how much information you have access to. Wikipedia is seen as the encyclopedia of the 21st century, but the quantity of information available differs for each language. This visualization of languages with Wikipedia articles shows the dynamic fluctuation in Wikipedia articles (x), users (y) and speakers (radius) of over 200 languages over the past 9 years. Languages are colored by geographic region; languages such as English or Spanish, which are spoken in multiple regions, are colored by where they originated. Mouseover to see more information!

The data for this visualization was collected from Wikipedia metadata and research.

We were inspired by Mike Bostock's visualization of the Wealth and Health of Nations, which in turn was based off of Hans Rosling's 2006 TED talk.
