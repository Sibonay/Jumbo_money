Languages & Information Availability

Mouseover the year to move forward and backwards through time.

Source: Tom Carden, Gapminder.

The languages you speak vastly affect the information you have access to. This visualization of languages supported by Wikipedia shows the dynamic fluctuation in Wikipedia articles (x), users (y) and speakers (radius) of over 200 languages over the past 9 years. Languages are colored by geographic region; when they are spoken in multiple regions, e.g. Spanish, they are colored by where they originated. Mouseover to see more information!

The data for this visualization was collected from various Wikipedia meta-articles and Wikipedia research.

We were inspired by Mike Bostock's visualization of the Wealth and Health of Nations, which in turn was based off of Hans Rosling's 2006 TED talk.
